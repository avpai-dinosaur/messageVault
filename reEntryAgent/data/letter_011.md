# Letter 011 - January 2025

Dear Case Manager,

Happy New Year. I spent the first week of this year rewriting my goals in plain language, trying to remove anything vague or performative. I want every line in my plan to be something I can execute, measure, and defend.

I reviewed your latest plan revision, and I want to thank you for incorporating some of my earlier comments. I can see the entrepreneurship language is beginning to show up more clearly. That said, I still feel the structure implies that "real success" means full-time traditional employment, while my documentary nonprofit path is treated like a side project. For me, it is the opposite.

To make this concrete, here is the framing I believe reflects my real priorities:

- Stability track: temporary work, housing compliance, parole reporting
- Growth track: filmmaking portfolio, U-M academic pathway, nonprofit design
- Contribution track: mentorship, community storytelling, re-entry support

These are not competing tracks. They are integrated, with entrepreneurship at the center and employment supporting it.

Parole update: I heard my hearing packet is complete pending one final verification item related to interstate supervision. I am hopeful but cautious. If there are recommended talking points for the next board interaction, I would appreciate guidance.

The U-M book project is now in full draft form. I reached the point where everything exists in rough shape, and now I am in revision mode. I added a chapter about learning to ask for help without surrendering agency. That chapter includes a section on working with case management in good faith while still advocating for my own direction.

I keep thinking about Bart Weiss and what I would ask if I got ten minutes with him. I wrote the questions down so I would be prepared:

1. What mistakes do first-time documentary storytellers from nontraditional backgrounds make most often?
2. How do you build credibility when you are unknown and resource-limited?
3. What is one thing I should do in my first year out to prove I belong in this field?

If there is any ethical and appropriate way to seek a meeting, even through a public event where I introduce myself briefly, I would like to pursue it.

I know there is still a lot outside my control. But my discipline is inside my control. My preparation is inside my control. My honesty about what I want is inside my control.

Thank you again for your support and for engaging with my feedback instead of dismissing it.

Respectfully,
Marcus Thompson
