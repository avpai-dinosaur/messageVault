# Letter 001 - March 2024

Dear Case Manager,

I hope this letter finds you well. I've been doing a lot of thinking lately about what comes next when I get out. It's been a long road, but finishing my social work degree here has given me so much clarity about what I want to do with my life.

I've been watching some films in the library - they let us watch documentaries on Thursdays - and it got me thinking. I never thought about filmmaking before, but there's something powerful about telling stories that can change people's lives. I saw this documentary about formerly incarcerated people starting their own businesses, and it just clicked for me. That's what I want to do.

I know this might sound random, but I've been reading about how people make films with just their phones now. iPhones, specifically. Is that really possible? Can you really make professional-looking documentaries with just a phone? I don't have any experience with this stuff, but I'm eager to learn.

Also, I wanted to ask about something more immediate - what's the process for getting a phone when I get out? Do I need to have a job first? Can someone cosign for me? I know I'll need one to stay connected with my parole officer and potential employers, but I'm not sure where to start.

The weather's been terrible here lately. We haven't been able to go outside for yard time in almost a week. It's making everyone restless. I've been using the time to read more about social work practices and community development.

Looking forward to hearing from you soon.

Best regards,
Marcus Thompson
