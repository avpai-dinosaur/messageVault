# Letter 004 - June 2024

Dear Case Manager,

I've been thinking more about that nonprofit idea I mentioned briefly before. What if I could start a nonprofit organization that teaches filmmaking to people coming out of prison? Or to at-risk youth? I feel like I could combine my social work background with filmmaking and really make a difference.

I know that's a big dream, and I'm getting ahead of myself since I haven't even learned how to make films yet. But it's something that excites me and gives me hope. Do you know anything about how to start a nonprofit? What kind of requirements there are? Would having a felony record prevent me from running one?

I've been reading everything I can find in the library about documentary filmmaking. There was this article about how smartphones - especially iPhones - have democratized filmmaking. Professional directors are actually using them now. That seems like something that could work for people without a lot of money. You don't need tens of thousands of dollars in equipment anymore.

The job workshop I mentioned last time was really helpful. They taught us how to write resumes and talk about our skills in positive ways. The instructor said my social work degree is a real asset and I should emphasize it. But she also said I shouldn't be afraid to talk about new interests and show that I'm someone who keeps learning.

We had a visit from some college students last week who are doing research on prison education programs. It was nice to talk to people from outside for a change. They were impressed that I got my degree while incarcerated. One of them told me about online learning platforms that offer free courses. I wrote down some names to look into when I get out.

My sister sent me some photos of her garden. She's really good at growing vegetables. It made me realize I haven't seen a real garden in almost two decades.

Thanks for all your help with these questions.

Best,
Marcus Thompson
