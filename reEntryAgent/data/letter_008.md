# Letter 008 - October 2024

Dear Case Manager,

I wanted to update you after the parole board prep session and share where my head is right now.

First, the hearing status: I was told my initial board appearance is still expected this cycle, but there may be a continuation if they want additional documentation on my release residence and support plan. I am trying not to panic about that. I know delays do not always mean denial, but it is hard not to hear every uncertainty as bad news when you have waited this long.

I have started organizing everything they might ask for into a clean packet mindset: release address options, support contacts, educational goals, and a practical daily schedule. If there is a template for what the board likes to see in these situations, I would be grateful for it.

On the re-entry plan, I still want to push for one major adjustment: the order of priorities. Employment should be in the plan, absolutely, but as a stabilizer while I build toward entrepreneurship. The current draft reads like employment is my long-term identity, and that is not true to what I have consistently told you.

To show I am serious, I took another step on the U-M application book project. I drafted a preface and one full chapter this month. The chapter is about the difference between being supervised and being supported. I wrote about how systems often measure compliance but not growth. I want the book to show admissions that I can think critically and write from lived experience without sounding bitter.

I am also collecting short reflections from my mentorship role here. Not names or private details, just moments that taught me something about communication, trust, and accountability. I think these stories can serve as both writing samples and seeds for documentary concepts.

About Bart Weiss: my interest is still strong. If there is any way to identify a public talk, workshop, or event where I could introduce myself after release, I would like to plan around that. I do not want to force access. I just want to be intentional and prepared if an appropriate opportunity comes up.

One more thing I want your input on: should the parole hearing narrative mention my nonprofit vision directly, or should I keep it narrower and focus on immediate compliance? My instinct is to do both, but with balance. I want the board to hear that I have ambition, but not in a way that sounds unrealistic on day one.

I am trying to live in that middle place between hope and discipline. Some days that is easier than others. Thank you for staying in this process with me.

With appreciation,
