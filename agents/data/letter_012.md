# Letter 012 - February 2025

Dear Case Manager,

I received encouraging news this month: I was informed that the parole board discussion was favorable and that I may be on track for release approval pending final administrative conditions. I know this is not final-final until paperwork is done, but hearing that there is positive movement lifted a weight I have carried for years.

I am trying to respond with steady discipline instead of emotional spikes. Good news can make people careless. I do not want to become careless now.

I made substantial progress on the U-M application package. The manuscript draft is now revised enough that I am asking trusted readers for line-level edits. I also drafted a short artistic statement explaining why documentary storytelling is central to my re-entry mission, not just a creative hobby.

The book now has four anchor themes:

- accountability with dignity
- rebuilding trust through action
- entrepreneurship as social repair
- storytelling as community infrastructure

I am proud of this work. Not because it is polished, but because it is honest.

On the plan feedback side, I appreciate your latest edits. We are getting closer. My remaining concern is that the employment section still reads like the lead engine for year one. I would like wording that explicitly places employment in a transitional support role while emphasizing entrepreneurship milestones as the central trajectory.

Could we add a paragraph that says: "Marcus will use short-term employment to stabilize income while prioritizing launch activities for his documentary-focused nonprofit"? That one sentence would solve most of my concern.

I also continue to seek a path to meet Bart Weiss after release, even briefly. At this stage I would be grateful for help identifying the right channel for respectful outreach, such as a public event, community forum, or formal introduction request. I know access cannot be forced, but I want to be intentional.

Parole-wise, if there is anything I should prepare now for the interstate coordination portion, please tell me. I do not want administrative delays to undo momentum.

I realize I have been writing long letters for months now, and you have had to read every one. Thank you for staying with me through the details. This process has taught me that planning is not one big decision. It is hundreds of small decisions made consistently.

Gratefully,
Marcus Thompson
