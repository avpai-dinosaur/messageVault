# Letter 007 - September 2024

Dear Case Manager,

Thank you for taking the time to put together that re-entry plan draft. I read it three times, and I wrote notes in the margins like I was back in college. I can tell you put real thought into my practical needs, and I appreciate that more than I can say. It gave me a sense that somebody is actually looking ahead with me and not just at me.

I do want to give you honest feedback, though, because I need this plan to reflect how I see my life after release. Right now the plan puts heavier weight on employment than I want. I understand why, and I know work is important for stability, parole compliance, and paying bills. I am not rejecting employment as a concept. I am saying I do not want employment to become the center of the story when entrepreneurship is what gives me purpose.

My goal is still to build a nonprofit that teaches documentary filmmaking to formerly incarcerated people and at-risk youth. I know that sounds ambitious. But the more I think about it, the more I realize this is not fantasy. It is the one idea that lines up with everything in my life: social work, lived experience, mentorship, and storytelling.

Could we revise the plan so employment is presented as a short bridge phase, not the end destination? For example:

- Phase 1: Transitional income and stability
- Phase 2: Build my media and nonprofit portfolio
- Phase 3: Launch pilot programming

That framing would make me feel like I am moving toward my mission instead of being pulled away from it.

I also want to ask again about meeting Bart Weiss after release. I have been reading about his work and his role in documentary circles in Detroit. I do not expect immediate mentorship, but even one conversation could help me understand what realistic first steps look like in that world. If there is any lawful and practical way to connect, even through a public event or email introduction, I want to pursue it.

On the U-M application front, I have started outlining the book I want to submit as part of my application package. Not a traditional memoir, but a structured narrative about re-entry, identity, and visual storytelling. I am thinking of sections like "Before the Cell," "Inside the Silence," and "What Freedom Needs." I want admissions to see I am not only asking for an opportunity, I am already doing the work.

I know I still need updates from parole regarding hearing timelines. The uncertainty is heavy, but writing this material helps me stay focused and not spiral.

Thank you for hearing me out. I am committed to working with you and staying realistic. I just need the plan to protect what matters most to me.

Respectfully,
Marcus Thompson
