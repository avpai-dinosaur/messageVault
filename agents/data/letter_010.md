# Letter 010 - December 2024

Dear Case Manager,

I am writing after hearing mixed news about the parole board timeline. I was told my review is moving, but the final decision window may extend into early next cycle because they want complete verification of support logistics. I will not pretend that did not hit me hard. For a day or two I felt like all momentum disappeared.

Then I came back to the routine. That is what keeps me grounded.

I continued building the U-M application book and completed another chapter focused on accountability after incarceration. I wrote about how accountability has to be active, not performative. Not just saying "I changed," but showing the systems, habits, and relationships that keep me on course. I think that chapter may be the strongest one yet.

I also started collecting feedback from peers here on readability. I ask them one question: "What part feels true and what part feels like I am trying too hard?" Their comments have been helpful. People can tell when writing comes from pressure instead of purpose.

On the plan you shared, I appreciate the detailed employment pathways and the practical contacts you included. But I need to repeat my central concern: the plan still leans too heavily toward employment as identity. For me, employment is strategy, not destination.

Could we revise language in each section so entrepreneurship is visibly primary? For instance:

- Immediate goal: income stabilization
- Core goal: nonprofit and documentary initiative development
- Long-term goal: sustainable social impact enterprise

That keeps me realistic while still honoring my mission.

I also want to check in on the possibility of meeting Bart Weiss after release. I am not asking for a favor beyond what is reasonable. I am asking for direction on how to approach a respected person in the field professionally and with humility. I want to be careful not to come across as entitled.

If parole timing slips, I plan to use that time to complete the manuscript draft fully, including a final section called "The First 180 Days." It will outline exactly how I plan to balance compliance, family reconnect, and entrepreneurial setup.

Even with uncertainty, I am not drifting. I am preparing.

Thank you for continuing to read these long letters and for not reducing me to a file number. That matters more than people realize.

Sincerely,
Marcus Thompson
