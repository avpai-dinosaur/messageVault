# Letter 006 - August 2024

Dear Case Manager,

I've been doing a lot of reflection as my release date approaches. Seven months to go now. It's surreal to think about.

I wanted to ask more specifically about the University of Michigan film school. I looked them up in an old college guide in the library, but the information was from 2019. Do you know if they have any programs for older students or people with non-traditional backgrounds? I'm wondering if my social work degree and my age would actually be advantages rather than disadvantages. I could bring a different perspective than the typical 22-year-old film student.

Do you think it's realistic to apply there? Would I need to have a portfolio of work? That's a chicken-and-egg problem if I've never made a film before. Maybe I should spend my first year out learning on my own and then apply? I could make some short documentaries about re-entry, about the challenges people face. That could be my portfolio.

This brings me back to the parole question I asked about earlier. If I'm going to apply to U of M and live in Ann Arbor with my sister, I really need to know if my Florida parole will allow that. It's keeping me up at night. The conditions of my parole say I need permission to leave the state for more than 48 hours, which suggests I might be stuck in Florida. But that doesn't make sense for my re-entry success - all my support is in Michigan.

I should probably get a lawyer, but I don't know how to find one or if I can afford one. Do you have resources for legal aid?

On a lighter note, I've been teaching some basic social work principles in my mentorship role here. Things like active listening, harm reduction, meeting people where they are. It's been rewarding. A few of the younger guys have said I've helped them see their situations differently.

My sister sent me an article about iPhone filmmaking that she printed out. She's been really supportive of this idea. That means a lot to me. She even said she'd help me get a phone when I first get out.

I can't believe I'm actually going to be free soon. It's terrifying and exciting at the same time.

Gratefully,
Marcus Thompson
